Pelvis Exercises

Kegels

  • Sit, stand, or lie down comfortably.
  • Contract the muscles you would use to stop the flow of urine. Avoid squeezing the buttocks or thighs.
  • Hold the contraction for 5-10 seconds, then release.
  • Relax for an equal amount of time before repeating.
  • Aim to complete 10-15 repetitions, gradually increasing the hold time as you progress.

Kegel exercises are simple yet highly effective for strengthening the pelvic floor muscles. These muscles support the bladder, uterus, and bowels, and play a crucial role in childbirth and postpartum recovery. To perform Kegels, you can sit, stand, or lie down comfortably. Contract the muscles you would use to stop the flow of urine, without squeezing the buttocks or thighs. Hold the contraction for 5-10 seconds, then release and relax for an equal amount of time. Aim to complete 10-15 repetitions, gradually increasing the hold time as you progress. Bridge Pose

  • Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart.
  • Engage your pelvic floor muscles and lift your hips towards the ceiling, creating a straight line from your shoulders to your knees.
  • Hold the bridge position for a few seconds, then lower your hips back to the floor.
  • Perform 10-15 repetitions, focusing on engaging the pelvic floor muscles throughout the movement.

The bridge pose is a gentle yoga exercise that targets the pelvic floor muscles while also engaging the glutes and core. Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. As you engage your pelvic floor muscles, lift your hips towards the ceiling, creating a straight line from your shoulders to your knees. Hold the bridge position for a few seconds, focusing on maintaining pelvic floor activation throughout the movement. Lower your hips back to the floor with control. Perform 10-15 repetitions, emphasizing proper form and pelvic floor engagement.

Squats

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
  • Engage your pelvic floor muscles as you lower your body into a squat position, as described earlier.
  • Focus on maintaining pelvic floor activation throughout the squat.
  • Return to the standing position, squeezing the glutes at the top.
  • Perform 10-15 repetitions, gradually increasing as tolerated.

Squats are an excellent lower body exercise that also engages the pelvic floor muscles. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out. As you lower your body into a squat position, engage your pelvic floor muscles to provide stability and support. Focus on maintaining proper form, keeping your back straight and knees aligned with your toes. Return to the standing position by pushing through your heels and squeezing the glutes at the top. Perform 10-15 repetitions, gradually increasing as tolerated. Squats can help strengthen the pelvic floor while also improving lower body strength and mobility during pregnancy.

Standing Pelvic Floor Contractions

  • Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hands on your hips or against a wall for support.
  • Engage your pelvic floor muscles as if lifting them upwards.
  • Hold the contraction for 5-10 seconds, then release.
  • Repeat for 10-15 repetitions, focusing on the quality of the contraction.

Standing pelvic floor contractions are a convenient exercise that can be done anywhere to strengthen the pelvic floor muscles. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hands on your hips or against a wall for support. Engage your pelvic floor muscles as if lifting them upwards. Hold the contraction for 5-10 seconds, then release and relax. Repeat for 10-15 repetitions, focusing on the quality of the contraction. Standing pelvic floor contractions help improve pelvic floor awareness and can be integrated into daily activities for ongoing pelvic health during pregnancy.

Hip Circles

  • Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hands on your hips.
  • Engage your pelvic floor muscles and gently rotate your hips in a circular motion, first clockwise and then counterclockwise.
  • Focus on maintaining pelvic stability and control throughout the movement.
  • Perform 10-15 circles in each direction.

Hip circles are a dynamic exercise that helps improve mobility in the hips while also engaging the pelvic floor muscles. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hands on your hips. Engage your pelvic floor muscles and gently rotate your hips in a circular motion, first clockwise and then counterclockwise. Focus on maintaining pelvic stability and control throughout the movement. Perform 10-15 circles in each direction, emphasizing smooth and controlled movement. Hip circles are a great way to increase circulation to the pelvic region and relieve tension during pregnancy.
